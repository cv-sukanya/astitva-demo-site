A Life of Dignity

Employment

A Chinese proverb states, “If you give a man a fish, you provide a meal; but, if you teach a man how to fish, you provide him a vocation and a means of livelihood”. Astitva provides vocational training and skill development, which has led to multiple employment options.

Creating Opportunities

With a mission to set up a thriving cotton industry in the heart of Shirpur, Astitva continues to provide employment and growth to over 5,000 locals. Employees are trained with skills, creating competence among them. The opportunity for further development is even offered.

Transformed Lives

Livelihood is what one searches for all their life. A stable source of income and good living conditions can go a long way. This is proven through the marvellous transformation of Shirpur, which has benefited thousands of families.

Skill Development Programs

In many communities, people struggle to find jobs because they lack the right skills. Astitva’s skill development programs offer training in areas like tailoring and advanced healthcare. These courses help participants learn valuable skills that prepare them for jobs or help them start small businesses. For example, women trained in tailoring can now create and sell clothes, while healthcare training enables others to assist in hospitals or clinics. These programs give people the confidence and tools to build a better future for themselves and their families​.

Entrepreneurship Awareness Program

Turning ideas into successful businesses is often a big challenge for young people in rural areas. Astitva, in collaboration with Hindustan Unilever Limited and NIESBUD, started the Entrepreneurship Awareness Program to guide youth in this journey. Through workshops and mentorship, participants learn how to develop business plans, manage finances, and grow their ideas into real businesses. This program not only provides practical knowledge but also inspires confidence and ambition, helping young minds take their first steps toward becoming successful entrepreneurs​.

Flour Mill Distribution

In remote areas, women often had to walk long distances just to grind flour, losing valuable time and energy. Astitva addressed this issue by setting up flour mills in underserved communities. These mills not only reduced the physical strain for women but also created opportunities for them to run and manage the mills as small businesses. By earning an income through this initiative, women gained independence and contributed to the well-being of their families. This simple solution brought big changes, turning everyday struggles into new opportunities​.
